  No need to reinvent the wheel, toothbrush or

   In markdown you can use inline math e.g. $...$ or match blocks e.g.
$$ ... $$ See the pandoc or kramdown converter for details [2].
Example:


Named mathematics operators are usually typeset in roman. Most of the stand=
ards
are already available. Some examples: $\det A$, $\cos\pi$, and $\log(1-x)$.

When an equation becomes too large to run in-line, you display it in a math=
=C2=9D
paragraph by itself.

$$
f(x) =3D 5x^{10}-9x^9 + 77x^8 + 12x^7 + 4x^6 - 8x^5 + 7x^4 + x^3 -2x^2 + 3x=
 + 11.
$$

   Looks familar?  It's just tex - surprise, surprise - nothing works
better for math in text. Use a free JavaScript library such as MathJax
to convert it to "typeset" formulas in HTML.

   Cheers.
