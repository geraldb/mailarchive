Hello,

> Awesome, is this like an open-source Prince?

  The tool uses WebKit (shipping with Qt) as its rendering engine,
and, thus supports HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, SVG, etc.

   However, WebKit doesn't support yet the CSS3 Paged Media Module[1],
that is supported by Prince and, thus, you cannot, for example, use
@page rules in your CSS:

@page
{
   background: url( gradients.svg );
}

  That would be handy to get a PDF page covered from top-to-bottom
without any margins left over with the gradient of your choice.

> Could you apply print stylesheets in order to style the PDF further?

 Using the --print-media-type switch applies the print stylesheet and
you can style it further as you like it   or you can just use a
regular stylesheet without the --print-media-type switch and remove
the JavaScript that hides and shows the slides.

   Cheers.

[1] http://www.w3.org/TR/css3-page/
