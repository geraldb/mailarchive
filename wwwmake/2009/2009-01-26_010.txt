Technically, maruku also gets it right by producing the same output.
The only difference is that it simply warns you (on STDERR) of
ambiguity, whereas the other Markdown processors do not.  For example,
if I silence the STDERR stream, I see only the wanted output:

# echo 'Ask not [...] what you can do for your country.' | maruku 2>/dev/null
<p>Ask not <span>&#8230;</span> what you can do for your country.</p>

I personally prefer the warning because it lets me catch spelling
mistakes I might have made or adds/deletes/edits I may have forgotten
to make while revising my documents.
