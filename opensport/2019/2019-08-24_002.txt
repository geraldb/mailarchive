   Welcome to football.db. Again thanks for your contributions.  Yes,
the repo gets updated mostly by hand - the idea is to make it work
like wikipedia / like a wiki and over time add more information e.g.
goals, etc.

  For auto-generated / auto-updated / auto-formated match datafiles
there's the sister project called football.csv, see
https://github.com/footballcsv    so that might a better fit for your
ideas / scripts.

  For now the football.csv get sourced from Joseph Buchdahl's Football
Data website
that gets updated twice a week and includes the secunda division too
(as far as I can remember), see
https://github.com/yorobot/football.csv/tree/master/sportdb-source-footballdata

   Sorry that I don't have an easy yes or no answer.  Yes,
auto-updates are welcome for football.csv  (but for now not for
football.db) - in the next months the football.csv will, of course,
sync and double-check hand-edited datafiles - but sorry for now the
focus is on improving the football.csv  auto-update infrastructure.

   The football.csv format is designed for automation and processing
