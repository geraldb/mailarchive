  Good question.

> But how is it going to update your DB in "real time"?

   Sorry. I do not want to disappoint you, but from the FAQ [1]:

>> football.db does NOT OFFER ANY REAL-TIME LIVE football results / scores services.

   As said you can always host your own service. To update the DB in
"real time" just reload the csv file everytime it gets
changed/regenerated (that's all).

   For the sample API running on heroku - it gets updated once every
24 hours. One way might be to use a (Ruby) script that "patches" the
results in the schedule offline (on your machine/or server) and than
commits the changes to the git repo.

 Cheers

[1] http://openfootball.github.io/questions.html#services
