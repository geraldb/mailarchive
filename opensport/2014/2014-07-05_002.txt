Hello,

   Great stuff. There's currently no concept of stages or phases in
the fixture parser. However, there are some alternatives.

   The easiest - use comments to mark the stage (of course, the
comment will not get added to the database) e.g.

 # Qualifying

or

# Kockout phase

or

## Qualifying stage ....     - everything after # or ## or ### is free
comment text.

   All rounds (regular or knockout) are defined in the language
mappings e.g. for English  [1] it is:

round:      Round|Matchday

playoffs:       Playoff|Playoffs|Play-off|Play-offs|Play-off for quarter-finals
round32:        Round of 32|Last 32
round16:        Round of 16|Last 16|8th finals
quarterfinals:  Quarterfinals|Quarter-finals|Quarters|Quarterfinal|Last 8
semifinals:     Semifinals|Semi-finals|Semis|Last 4
fifthplace:     Fifth place match|Fifth place|5th place match|5th
place final|5th place|Match for fifth place|Match for 5th place
thirdplace:     Third place match|Third-place match|Third place|3rd
place match|3rd place final|3rd place|Match for third place|Match for
3rd place|Third-place play-off|Third place play-off
final:          Final|Finals


  To define/use your own "custom" round as a workaround for now you can use

(1)  Round  //  Qualifying    # e.g. round num - round workaround
after // the "real name" for your round.

  If you collect some missing round (names) that you need I can try to
add them to parser w/ an upcoming update. Keep it up. Cheers.


[1] github.com/openfootball/lang/blob/master/en.yml
